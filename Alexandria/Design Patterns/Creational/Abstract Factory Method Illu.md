- The essence of the Abstract Factory Pattern is to 
  "Provide an interface for creating families of related or dependent objects 
  without specifying their concrete classes."   

- For ProductA, we have 2 version, one is FancyProductA and the other is ModernProductA.
  The same applied to ProductB
- We want to group them in their respective family, for our example, it is "Fancy" and "Modern",
  this is why we added another layer of abstraction for our product.
- The extra layer allow us to group previously separated factory method into class,
